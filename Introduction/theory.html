The invention of Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) is credited to Håkon Wium Lie, a Norwegian web pioneer. In October 1994, while working at CERN alongside Tim Berners-Lee, Lie proposed CSS as a solution to separate content from presentation on the web. This separation allowed for more flexible and maintainable web design by enabling multiple style sheets to influence the presentation of a document through a cascading mechanism.
